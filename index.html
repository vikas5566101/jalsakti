About Heavy Metal Pollution Index (HMPI)

The Heavy Metal Pollution Index is a comprehensive method for assessing groundwater quality based on heavy metal concentrations. It considers the relative importance of different metals and their potential health impacts.

HMPI Categories:

  • Safe (HMPI ‚â§ 100): Water is suitable for consumption with minimal health risks
  • Moderate (100 < HMPI ‚â§ 200): Water requires treatment before consumption
  • Hazardous (HMPI > 200): Water is unsuitable for consumption and poses significant health risks

How to Use:

  1. Enter sample data manually or upload a CSV/JSON file
  2. Include heavy metal concentrations in mg/L
  3. Optionally provide GPS coordinates for mapping
  4. Calculate HMPI and view results in various formats
